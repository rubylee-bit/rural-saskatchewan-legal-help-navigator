Immigration and government contacts

Federal Immigration

IRCC Client Support Centre

Use IRCC for general federal immigration questions, application status, technical account issues, and official immigration webform inquiries.

  • Call: 1-888-242-2100
  • Have your Unique Client Identifier, or UCI, ready if you have one.
  • Calling around opening time may help, but lines can be busy.
  • IRCC agents cannot speed up processing or give legal immigration advice.
Contact IRCC Use IRCC Webform
Provincial Immigration

Government of Saskatchewan Immigration / SINP

For Saskatchewan Immigrant Nominee Program questions, contact the provincial government rather than federal IRCC agents.

  • Call: 1-833-613-0485
  • Email: immigration@gov.sk.ca
  • Employer technical issues: employerservices@gov.sk.ca
Visit Saskatchewan Immigration

Yorkton and rural newcomer support

Yorkton Newcomer Support

East Central Newcomer Welcome Centre

The East Central Newcomer Welcome Centre provides newcomer and settlement support in Yorkton and the surrounding region.

  • Phone: (306) 783-2777
  • Email: ecnwcinfo@ecnwc.ca
  • May assist with settlement documents, housing orientation, translation, and regional support.
  • Serves a large area around Yorkton, including smaller surrounding communities.
Visit Newcomer Centre
Rural Access

Remote and Satellite Support

Newcomers in surrounding rural communities may be able to access support without travelling to Yorkton.

  • Remote support may be available by phone, Zoom, or email.
  • Settlement staff may travel to smaller towns and municipal buildings.
  • Communities such as Melville, Esterhazy, Canora, Kamsack, and Langenburg may be served remotely.
Ask About Rural Support
Schools

Settlement Workers in Schools

Settlement Workers in Schools, or SWIS, can help immigrant families connect with school systems and local supports.

  • Support may be available directly through rural school divisions.
  • Helpful for families with children moving into smaller rural communities.
  • Can assist families with integration into local school networks.
Learn About SWIS

Language and document support

English Classes

LINC and ESL Support

Language support may help newcomers improve English for work, citizenship, permanent residency, and everyday life.

  • Language Instruction for Newcomers to Canada, or LINC
  • ESL support through local providers such as Suncrest College
  • An English level assessment may be required before beginning classes.
Visit Suncrest College
Translation

Document Translation and Interpretation

Translation and interpretation support may be useful when dealing with official applications, health care, legal matters, or other daily tasks.

  • Official document translation may be available through the Newcomer Centre.
  • Interpretation support may be available through the Newcomer Centre.
  • English conversation circles and community matching may also be offered.
Contact the Newcomer Centre

Watch for immigration fraud

Immigration scams can target people who are stressed, waiting for applications, or unsure where to get help. Use official government websites and be cautious if something feels suspicious.

  • Legitimate fees are not paid by cryptocurrency, gift cards, prepaid credit cards, Western Union, or personal bank transfer.
  • Official IRCC communications should come from government email addresses ending in .gc.ca.
  • Be cautious of messages that pressure you to act quickly or create panic.
  • IRCC will not call unexpectedly to sell immigration packages or special processing deals.

Immigration issues may overlap with other problems.

Newcomers may need support with employment, housing, language, school systems, mental health, or legal information at the same time. It is okay to use more than one section of this website.
