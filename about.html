About the Rural Access to Justice Saskatchewan Project

The Rural Saskatchewan Help Navigator is part of a broader student-led initiative focused on improving access to legal and community information in rural Saskatchewan.

About the Project

The Rural Saskatchewan Help Navigator is part of the Rural Access to Justice Saskatchewan Project, a student-led initiative that aims to help residents, particularly those in rural communities across the province, find the right resources for legal help.

The barriers that exist in these areas are not limited to the lack of legal resources in smaller communities, but also to the fact that many people often do not know where to start or how to get the proper, tailored help for their situation.

One of the major differences between large urban centres and rural communities is that there might not be a large law firm that is accessible just down the street for residents to find. There are fewer advertisements for legal help and fewer resources, such as day-to-day information that gets passed on within communities, leading many residents to not know where to start with their situation.

Furthermore, with limited access, the right kind of help can be difficult to identify, which is what this website seeks to bridge. Different from other legal websites, this website puts all the relevant information regarding legal help in one place and breaks down the necessary steps, from the very start to where to get help, in a simplified manner, directing people to official organizations that can provide further assistance.

Ultimately, this project is founded on a simple belief: everyone deserves to understand where they can turn for help, regardless of where they live.

The Approach

The Rural Saskatchewan Help Navigator has been designed around a simple idea: finding the right help should not be complicated.

  • Provide public legal information in one accessible location.
  • Connect users with official organizations and community services.
  • Present information in a clear and easy-to-navigate format.
  • Recognize that legal, housing, employment, immigration, and mental health issues often overlap.
  • Offer a free and publicly accessible starting point for anyone seeking help.

About the Creator

This project was created by Ruby, a student at the University of Toronto with an interest in improving access to justice in rural communities. Through research and community-focused work, the goal of this initiative is to make trusted public legal information easier to access by bringing together legal and community resources in one practical and accessible place.

Disclaimer

The Rural Saskatchewan Help Navigator provides public legal information only. It does not provide legal advice and should not be considered a substitute for speaking with a lawyer or another qualified professional. Users should verify information directly with the relevant organizations, as services, policies, contact information, and eligibility requirements may change over time.
