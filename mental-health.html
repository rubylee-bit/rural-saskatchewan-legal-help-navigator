Mental Health & Addictions

Find crisis lines, mental health services, addictions supports, youth supports, Indigenous supports, and Yorkton-area resources.

This page provides public information about mental health and addictions supports. It is not medical advice, counselling, or a substitute for speaking with a qualified professional. Resource availability, hours, and eligibility may change, so contact each organization directly to confirm current information.

If you need immediate help

If there is immediate danger or a medical emergency, call 911 or go to the nearest emergency department.

911

Immediate danger or emergency.

988

Call or text the 24/7 Suicide Crisis Helpline.

811

Youth, Indigenous, and culturally relevant supports

Youth Support

Kids Help Phone

Kids Help Phone is a national 24/7 e-mental health service offering free, confidential, and multilingual support for youth.

  • Call: 1-800-668-6868
  • Minors can text: 686868
  • Adults can text: 741741
  • Phone, text, and live chat support may be available
Visit Kids Help Phone
Indigenous Support

Hope for Wellness Hotline

The Hope for Wellness Hotline provides 24/7 telephone support for Indigenous people.

  • Call: 1-855-242-3310
  • Available: 24/7
  • Telephone support available in English, French, Cree, Ojibway, and Inuktitut
Visit Hope for Wellness

Not sure what kind of support to look for?

Mental health, addictions, housing, employment, legal issues, family safety, and financial stress can overlap. It is okay if you are not sure which category fits.

  • If someone is in immediate danger, call 911.
  • If this is suicide-related or an emotional crisis, call or text 988.
  • If you need health advice, call 811.
  • If you need help finding community resources, call 211.
  • If your issue overlaps with legal, housing, employment, or immigration concerns, use the Start Here page.
