Housing & Employment

Stable housing and fair employment are important foundations for personal well-being and access to justice.

This page provides public information about housing, tenant, employment, and basic rights resources in Saskatchewan. It is a starting point only and does not replace legal advice, government information, or professional support.

Know your rights

In Saskatchewan, workers and job seekers have legal protections. If you are unsure whether your rights have been violated, Employment Standards may be able to explain the complaint process.

  • Employers and recruitment agencies cannot charge job seekers money to find work.
  • Employers pay recruiters; job seekers should not be charged recruitment fees.
  • Workers have rights related to wages, breaks, hours of work, and final pay.
  • Some complaints can be handled remotely through the online complaint process.

Housing and employment often overlap.

Losing work, unsafe housing, rent stress, mental health, immigration, and legal problems can affect each other. It is okay to look at more than one section of this website.

  • Losing a job may affect housing, finances, and mental health.
  • Housing insecurity may make it harder to work or attend appointments.
  • Newcomers may face employment, housing, language, and legal barriers at the same time.
